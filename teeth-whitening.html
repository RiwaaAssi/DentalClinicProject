Treatment Steps

This is the first item's accordion body. Evaluate the patient’s dental health and determine if they are a good candidate for teeth whitening. Discuss the patient’s desired outcome and explain the different whitening options available (in-office vs. at-home treatments).

This is the second item's accordion body.Clean the teeth thoroughly to remove any plaque, tartar, and surface stains. Document the current shade of the teeth to compare the results after treatment.

This is the third item's accordion body.Place protective barriers on the gums and soft tissues to prevent irritation from the whitening agent. Provide the patient with protective eyewear if using a laser or light-activated whitening system.

This is the forth item's accordion body.Apply the whitening gel (usually containing hydrogen peroxide or carbamide peroxide) to the teeth. For in-office treatments, a special light or laser may be used to enhance the effectiveness of the whitening agent.

This is the fifth item's accordion body.Activate the whitening agent (if required) and monitor the teeth during the treatment. The whitening process may take 15-30 minutes, depending on the method used.

This is the sixth item's accordion body.Rinse the teeth and remove the protective barriers. Compare the final shade of the teeth with the initial shade to assess the results.

This is the seven item's accordion body.PAdvise the patient to avoid foods and drinks that can stain the teeth (such as coffee, tea, and red wine) for at least 24-48 hours. Recommend using a whitening toothpaste to help maintain the results. Schedule follow-up visits or provide take-home whitening kits if needed.

This is the eight item's accordion body.Schedule a follow-up appointment to check the results and ensure there are no issues such as sensitivity or irritation. Discuss the maintenance of whitening results and when to consider a touch-up treatment.
